Economic Development

Communities Cannot Progress Without Economic and Human Development.

Economic development is the backbone of long-term productive potential of any economy. Growth trends are an indication of Gross National Product and growth rate measures require series of data collected over years, as average growth rates peak during different economic cycles for each economy.

Several factors which affect the process of economic development:

  • Changes in real exchange rate;
  • Cyclical fluctuations in Gross National Product and foreign trade.
  • Financial instability, such as an unsustainable credit boom and decline in savings.
  • Fluctuation of world prices for basic imports and exports.
  • Military conflicts and political instability.
  • Natural disasters and other destabilizing events.
  • Drastic changes or mutations in the technology industry.
